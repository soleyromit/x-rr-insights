3-Level governance model Active design direction
Level 1 · CRITICAL
Settings that affect all tenants or break program-specific configurations. Requires senior approval + audit log. Example: changing global HIPAA field defaults.
Level 2 · Manager approval
Settings that affect a program or site. Surfaced in review queue. Manager must approve before applying. Example: changing clinical site form structure.
Level 3 · Warning only
Low-risk changes with in-context warning. User can proceed without approval. Example: reordering fields on a non-critical form section.

Stakeholder Deck
Executive-ready · Audience: Arun Gautam + Kunal (COO) · March 2026
Slide 1 · The problem
Exxat's five clinical education products serve 170+ accredited healthcare programs, but each product carries unresolved friction that compounds at scale. FaaS runs at 95,000 support tickets annually with a 2/5 NPS. Exam Management lacks the accessibility and multi-campus capabilities that competitors already provide. Skills Checklist cannot answer the question every student asks: "have I met my requirements?" These are not isolated product bugs — they are symptoms of missing platform-level architecture.
Slide 2 · What we found
Across 39 stakeholder sessions (March 2026), six platform-level signals emerged. Three are new: an AI opportunity layer confirmed across all five products, multi-campus fragmentation affecting an estimated 30–40% of the client base, and a standalone skills entity gap that blocks three personas simultaneously. The most urgent near-term finding: Touro runs seven survey types outside Exxat in Blue and Canvas because our survey UX lost their trust. That is recoverable, but only with a dedicated course evaluation module, not a patch.
Slide 3 · Recommended direction
Three actions with the highest cross-product leverage: (1) Ship the accessibility layer for Exam Management before the UNF pilot in July — this is also the platform's first public commitment to WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. (2) Begin the standalone skills entity architecture in Q2 — every day it delays, the SCCE and student personas remain underserved across two products at once. (3) Run the course evaluation module design workshop in April as planned — this is how Exxat gets back the survey surface it has already lost to competitors.
